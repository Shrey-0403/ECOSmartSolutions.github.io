Costs + Incentives of Green Solutions

There are so many ways to protect our planet.
See below for the cost of these various green solutions!

Solar Panels + LED Bulbs

  • The average cost for a solar panel system is $15,000
  • By installing a $15,000 system, you can save $22,500 per 15 years by investing in solar panels!

  • LED lighting: LED bulbs start at $15. LED lights go up to $300 based on the quality.
  • You'd need around 30 to light an entire mid-sized house.

Geothermal Power and Wind Power

  • The average cost for a geothermal heat pump system is $24,500
  • The average cost for a wind turbine is 2-4 million dollars
  • One wind turbine can pay itself back in about 6 years and will genearate electricty freely for the rest of its 25 year lifespan.

Tankless Heater + Home Appliances

  • Tankless Water Heater: $1000-2000 for the majority of good quality heaters
  • Only needs to be replaced every 15-20 years
  • Home appliances: Eco-friendly modern appliances, on average, only cost around 15% more than standard models, and will cost around $5,000
  • However, considering the fact that these appliances use 40% less energy, the hefty price is made up for with lower electricity costs.

Government Incentives:

To promote usage of green energy, the government has implemented many incentives to increase it's usage!

  • A federal tax credit of 30% is available to individuals who have installed solar panels or plan to do so within the next 10 years. In order to qualify for the tax credit, the installed system must have a minimum capacity of 2.3 kWh.
  • Individuals with geothermal heat pumps with an efficiency rating of at least 75% are eligible for a 26% to 30% tax credit. This renewable energy tax credit covers 30% of the total system cost, including installation, of the geothermal heat pump.
  • Individuals with larger residential biomass heating systems can claim 30% of their money spent in the form of tax credit with a cap of $2000 annually.
  • There is an incentive of up to $4000 when one purchases or leases a zero-emission vehicle in NJ. Individuals with newer cars and higher household incomes can also be eligible for a $7500 tax credit.
  • The renewable energy production tax Credit allows owners of wind energy to claim a federal tax credit on every kWh of electricity for the first 10 years. Currently, the credit is 2.6 cents per kWh.
